The Rocky Mountain Field Trip

Your first experience in the department will be a geology field trip right in your new back yard - the beautiful countryside of Wyoming. The Rocky Mountain Field Trip is how we kick things off in the department every acadmeic year. Here is a youtube video highlighting what to expect . The trip is a great place for new graduate students to meet faculty and current students and get insight into the geology of a part of Wyoming. This year, pandemic permitting, we will tour southwestern Wyoming for 5 days. Highlights will include camping at Flaming Gorge, prospecting for fossil fish, and visiting Boar's Tusk, an exposed volcanic plug.

Justice, Equity, Diversity, and Inclusivity

Like many STEM departments in the US, we have a long way to go on justice, equity, diversity, and inclusivity (JEDI) issues in G&G at UW. But we are trying to do better, engaging on a number of fronts to make our department's culture more inclusive. One example is the formation of a JEDI Committee in mid 2020 after the George Floyd and Breonna Taylor murders. The committee includes 5 faculty members and 12 students and postdocs. As a first step, we abandoned the GRE in grad admissions decisions and have since been developing an antiracist, antisexist, pro LGBTQAI+ action plan - part of a long term vision to improve the environment in the department, on campus, and in the surrounding community. In January 2021, as part of our multipronged, multiyear effort, we also joined the national Unlearning Racism in Geosciences (URGE) initiative.

World-Class Research Facilities

The department boasts a world class materials characterization facility, including top-notch XRF, XRD, and SEM instrumentation for interogating rocks, soils, and sediments. In addition we have state-of-the-art wet chemical analysis capabilities, including ICP-MS, ICP-OES, and ICS instrumentation, and a MC-ICPMS for isotopic analyses spanning much of the periodic table. The department also hosts a a world class Near Surface Geophysics facility with a wide array of equipment, instrumentation, and software for surface, marine, and borehole surveys of geophysical properties of Earth materials. In addition, several faculty members maintain facilities for specific applications such as cosmogenic nuclides, experimental geochemistry, and hydrogeophysical analyses. All of this is complemented by a cavernous rock prep room that is complete with crushers, grinders, saws, a Wilfley table - you name it, we've got it. We also have spacious wet chemistry labs throughout the building. Everything you need is right at your fingertips here in the department.
